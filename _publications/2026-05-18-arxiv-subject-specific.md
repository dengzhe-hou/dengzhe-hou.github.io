We investigate self-initiated attention shifts through subject-level EEG decoding using a machine learning–based approach with two complementary analyses: frequency-specific topographic patterns and SHapley Additive exPlanations (SHAP) feature attribution. Results show reliable within-subject classification performance, with higher-frequency bands and frontal regions contributing most to model decisions. We note caution regarding potential non-neural artifacts in high-frequency signals.

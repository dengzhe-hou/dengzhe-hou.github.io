We introduce a framework where clinicians and researchers direct skill-augmented AI agents through natural language to execute complex, multi-step biomedical workflows while maintaining oversight. The infrastructure comprises three layers: capable language models, agent frameworks (OpenClaw and Hermes Agent), and a curated medical skills collection with over 1,000 capabilities. Case studies demonstrate applications in rare disease diagnosis, drug repurposing, and clinical trial design, and we discuss key challenges including hallucination risks, data privacy, and over-reliance.

EEG deep learning predictions are unstable across preprocessing pipelines. Across six datasets spanning four paradigms, up to 42% of trial-level predictions flip when only the preprocessing changes — same data, same model, same subject. We introduce three contributions: (i) a Walsh-Hadamard decomposition that reveals near-additive sensitivity patterns across pipeline components, (ii) Preprocessing Uncertainty as a diagnostic metric for per-trial reliability, and (iii) Normalized Adaptive PGI as a regularization strategy that mitigates pipeline-induced instability.
